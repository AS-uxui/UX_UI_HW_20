Happy Travels App - Solving travellers problems

-a simple and easy to use app to plan your trips



Travel tools are useful to plan family, group, solo and business trips. These tools have replaced travel agents. Expanding global economies has created a huge customer base for travel. Currently, the tools available in the market are either catering to existing members as a service or are trying to expand their member base for ad revenue. Our research identified travellers need a simple, easy to use, single app to plan their trips. The wireframes created as part of this study helped me to understand “easy to use” or “simple” is achievable and requires a lot of usability testing.

Research

The Problem

There is no easy-to-use tool that supports travellers for a unique immersive experience.

The Solution

This solution offers the modern traveller the ability to plan their trips using a mobile app. It gives them the flexibility to change plans at their destination to suit needs and budget

My team conducted a research to understand the pain points of travellers. Qualitative data gleaned from interviews was paired with quantitative data generated from an e-survey.

Pain points:
  • Using too many tools to plan trips
  • Difficulty coordinating trips with others
  • Applications are difficult to navigate and run slowly

User Insight

A traveller on vacation with their young family needs a tool to plan family friendly activities because they want to deepen familial bonds as they explore and enjoy the experience.

Problem Statement



Travel tools are designed to help plan a trip, whether it is to compare prices, make reservations, and/or plan activities. Based on user research, we found that travellers use multiple tools, including google searches because there is no single tool that satisfies their planning needs. This is extremely frustrating for travellers. How might we build a travel app that facilitates access to pertinent information for the traveller in order to provide a positive travel experience ? How might we quantify the success of the app by the

  • number of return visits to app while on vacation to modify itinerary
  • customer review stars or thumbs up

Iteration Section

I iterated the wireframes and create a link to the set up alerts screen and a link to the coaching screen for alerts in the planning a trip screen. The user flow would logically take the user to the alerts screen when the have confirmed or paying for their trip. The participants in the usability tests would benefit from the alert accessible through a link or a sitemap.

I addressed the navigational difficulty of Task 5 in the guerilla usuability tests by iterating 2 wireframes (Confirm trip and Destination search). I added a way for the member to set up an alert when they choose to confirm a trip or search for a destination. I would also add a tooltip to the alert or a way to access the coaching screen from the tool tip in the final product. The clickable digital wireframe was modified to reflect the changes using inVision.
